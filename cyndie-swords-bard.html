The Swords Bard

Built with love for Cyndie — the name, the story, the soul are yours.

Hey Cyndie — we didn't want to hand you a blank page. This is a full build for a Level 5 Swords Bard, designed specifically for your table: Mike's structured adventures, a raucous party that loves chaos, and a character who feels woven into the story rather than waiting for her moment.

Every mechanical choice here has a reason. You'll see it explained as you go. The things left blank — your character's name, her history, her voice, what she looks like, what she wants — that's the canvas. We gave you the instrument. You play it.

This character doesn't sit in the corner waiting. She makes everyone around her better, and the table feels it every session.

Class Features
Bardic Inspiration
Bard · Core
As a bonus action, give one creature within 60 ft a d8 Bardic Inspiration die. They can add it to one ability check, attack roll, or saving throw within the next 10 minutes. You have 5 uses, and they recharge on a short or long rest (Font of Inspiration, level 5).
This is your primary engagement engine. Every turn someone rolls something important, you're in it. You hand Nick a d8 before his Big Spell. You hand Anthony a d8 before his critical attack. The table feels you there — in their moments, not just yours. 5 uses refreshing on short rest means you give it away freely.
Blade Flourish
College of Swords · Level 3
When you take the Attack action, your speed increases by 10 ft. If a weapon attack hits, you can expend a Bardic Inspiration die to add its roll to the damage and choose one flourish:
Defensive Flourish
Add the die to damage and to your AC until the start of your next turn. Great when you're surrounded or protecting an ally.
Slashing Flourish
Add the die to damage. Deal the same amount to another creature within 5 ft of the target. One swing, two enemies hurt.
Mobile Flourish
Add the die to damage. Push the target 5 + the die result feet away. You can then move to a space adjacent to them as a reaction. Ultimate repositioning tool.
Blade Flourish means every melee hit becomes a moment — extra damage, crowd control, or tactical repositioning. You're not just swinging, you're shaping the battlefield. Save Bardic Inspiration for flourishes when you need burst, and hand them to allies when control is more valuable than your damage spike.
Fighting Style: Dueling
College of Swords · Level 3
When you are wielding a melee weapon in one hand and no other melee weapons, you gain +2 to damage rolls with that weapon.
Simple and reliable. Every rapier hit does +2 damage. Free hand stays open for spellcasting components. No fiddling required.
Jack of All Trades
Bard · Level 2
Add half your proficiency bonus (+1) to any ability check you're not proficient in.
You're never useless on any check. In Mike's structured adventures with puzzles, traps, and knowledge challenges, this keeps you contributing constantly.
Song of Rest
Bard · Level 2
During a short rest, any friendly creatures who spend Hit Dice to recover HP can add 1d6 to each die spent.
You make short rests better for the whole party. Nobody has to be near death for this to matter — you're just quietly, constantly making everyone's survival easier.

15 ft cube of thunder from you. DC 16 CON or 2d8 thunder + pushed 10 ft. Loud, dramatic, and very satisfying when surrounded. The table will hear it.
Charm Person

DC 16 WIS or a humanoid views you as a friendly acquaintance for 1 hour. Ends if you or your companions harm them. Mike's structured adventures always have NPCs worth charming.
2nd Level
3 SLOTS
Hold Person
ControlConcentration
DC 16 WIS or a humanoid is paralyzed for up to 1 minute. All attacks against them have advantage. All hits within 5 ft are auto-crits. Cast this on a boss and Anthony doubles his damage. Powerful synergy with the whole party.
Shatter
Damage
10 ft sphere of thunderous concussive energy. DC 16 CON or 3d8 thunder damage. No concentration — just damage. Great for clustered enemies. Constructs and objects have disadvantage on the save.
Invisibility
UtilityConcentration
You or a willing creature becomes invisible for up to 1 hour. Ends if the target attacks or casts. Scouting, escape, setup. Incredibly flexible. Mike's adventures almost always have a use for this.
3rd Level
2 SLOTS
Hypnotic Pattern
ControlConcentration
30 ft cube of swirling patterns. DC 16 WIS or every creature is incapacitated — speed 0, can't take actions. Creatures stay incapacitated until the pattern ends or they take damage. When this lands on a group of enemies, the fight is essentially over. Save it for when it matters.
Hold Person and Hypnotic Pattern are your two big plays. They don't do damage — they create conditions where everyone else does damage twice as effectively. That's the bard's superpower: your spell makes Nick's spell hit harder, makes Anthony's axe crit, makes Denali's arrows all land. You're a force multiplier.

How to Play Her
⚔️
Round One — Read the Room
Open with Hypnotic Pattern if enemies are grouped and Mike doesn't have a ready counter. Open with Hold Person on the biggest threat if it's a single dangerous humanoid. Otherwise, hand out Bardic Inspiration and get in melee. You don't have to detonate immediately — sometimes setting up Anthony or Nick's big move is the highest value play.
🎭
Social Encounters — You Are the Party
Persuasion +11. Deception +8. You walk into every negotiation, every tense meeting, every interrogation as the one who talks first. Mike's structured adventures reward this. Use Charm Person when the words aren't landing. You don't need to dominate — just open the door and let the party walk through.
🎲
Bardic Inspiration — Give It Away
Five uses. Refreshes on short rest. Hand them out aggressively. Give Nick one when he's winding up a spell. Give Anthony one before a critical swing. Give Tanisha one before a wild shape contested check. The die unused is wasted. You're not hoarding power — you're a battery for the group.
🌀
Concentration — Protect It
Hold Person and Hypnotic Pattern both require concentration. Only one at a time. When you have a big control spell running, stay out of melee or use Defensive Flourish to boost your AC. If you get hit and fail concentration, the spell drops — position matters. Tanisha's Wild Shape forms make great bodyguards.
💫
In The In-Between — She Never Disappears
Between fights, between encounters — Vicious Mockery on a bandit for roleplay flavor, Mage Hand doing something useful or funny, Charm Person turning a tense standoff into an ally. Insight checks reading NPCs. Perception holding down the flanks. She stays present. That was the whole point.
